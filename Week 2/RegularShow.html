Regular Show is an American cartoon television show created by J. G. Quintel. It was created for Cartoon Network where it made its debut. The show is about the lives of a blue jay named Mordecai and a raccoon named Rigby, both of whom work at a local park as groundkeepers. Most episodes begin when the duo tries to solve simple work problems that leads to some form of supernatrual misadventure. While they attempt to get out of their sticky situations, they also interact with with the show's other main characters; Muscle Man, Skips, Pops, Benson, and Hi-Five Ghost. The show is considered controvertial because its adult jokes and crude themes.[4] As of May 2013, Regular Show had been consistently viewed by 2.5 million each week. The series has been praised by critics and continues to be watched by all ages. Regular Show has also been nominated for many awards, including seven Annie Awards, six Emmys (one of which it won) for the episode "Eggcellent" (season 3, episode 18) and three British Academy Children's Awards. Premise

The series revolves around the daily lives of two 23-year-old friends, a blue jay named Mordecai and a racoon named Rigby. They work at a park as groundskeepers and spend their time avoiding work. [10]This is much to the chagrin of their boss, Benson, an uptight gumball machine. Most of the other coworkers will acompany Mordecai and Rigby on their endeavors.
